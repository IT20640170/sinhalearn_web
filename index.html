About the System



SinhaLearn Research Project addresses a pressing issue that has Grade 5 scholaship students in Sri Lanka. The Sinhala language, deeply rooted in Pali and Sanskrit, holds immense cultural significance for the Sinhalese community in Sri Lanka. However, its intricate morphology and diglossic nature, where written and spoken forms diverge, pose significant challenges. In response, we present "SinhaLearn" an automated system designed specifically to enhance Sinhala language proficiency for the Grade 5 Scholarship examination domain. Our system leverages advanced Natural Language Processing (NLP) algorithms, Optical Character Recognition (OCR) technology, and Part-of-Speech (POS) tagging. Key functionalities include Sinhala Handwriting Capturing and Recognition, real-time error detection and correction in spelling and grammar within Sinhala Present Tense sentences, simplified explanations for complex Sinhala vocabulary aligned with the Grade 5 curriculum, and automated assessment of handwritten responses with instant feedback and scoring. Notably, informed by a comprehensive literature review, our research has made significant improvements by blending rule-based methods with hybrid components, effectively addressing critical gaps in the field. While existing systems often concentrate on singular functionalities such as spelling analysis, grammar analysis, dictionary functions, or content display, our "SinhaLearn" system provides a holistic solution by integrating these aspects. This comprehensive approach sets a new benchmark for automated systems in Sinhala language proficiency.

LITERATURE SURVEY

The exploration of Natural Language Processing (NLP) in the context of the Sinhala language reflects a journey into the intricacies and challenges posed by this rich linguistic landscape. Sinhala, as the official language of Sri Lanka, embodies a unique linguistic complexity stemming from its roots in Pali and Sanskrit. This complexity unfolds in both spoken and written forms, each governed by nuanced rules that contribute to the layers of intricacy. The formality of written Sinhala, relying on well-defined rules, adds another layer of challenge for comprehensive implementation within a single application. The scarcity of tools tailored for Sinhala, especially in spelling and grammar checking, prompted Abeyrathne et al. to introduce a web platform. This platform employs a two-pronged approach, utilizing both rule-based and data-driven methods for spell-checking. The components include a language model, error model, candidate model, and selection mechanism, showcasing an attempt to navigate the intricate spelling rules of Sinhala. Pabasara et al. delve into the realm of grammar error detection, presenting a hybrid model that combines rule-based and machine learning algorithms. The study harnesses the power of a Part-of-Speech (POS) tagger and a morphological analyzer to achieve an impressive overall accuracy of 88.6%. This blending of traditional and modern approaches emerges as a promising strategy for tackling the challenges of Sinhala grammar complexity.

Liyanage et al.'s computational grammar of Sinhala embarks on a mission to identify essential features, such as person, gender, number, and case, through the application of Context-Free Grammar and the Natural Language Tool Kit. The study, while demonstrating a 60% accuracy rate, emphasizes the need for further refinement of morphological analyzers and word segmentation algorithms to enhance precision. The hybrid part-of-speech tagger developed by the University of Colombo employs a combination of rule-based and stochastic techniques. This approach exhibits promising overall accuracy, addressing the limitations encountered with a purely rule-based method. It stands as a testament to the evolving strategies employed by researchers to optimize language processing for Sinhala.

Optical Character Recognition (OCR) techniques in the Sinhala language research landscape are gaining momentum. The Data-Driven spell checker's utilization of n-grams presents a notable contribution. This technique preprocesses input sentences, employing word frequency to provide accurate spelling suggestions, showcasing a data-driven approach in handling Sinhala text. The SinSpell system, utilizing the Hunspell engine, introduces a rule-based Spelling Error Detection and auto-correction module, distinguishing between correctly and incorrectly spelled words. This engine's rule-based approach, combined with the creation of dictionary and affix files, reflects a meticulous effort to improve spelling accuracy. Moreover, SinSpell's comprehensive nature extends to the error analysis of various documents, such as reports and letters. The study acknowledges the challenges posed by the complexity of the Sinhala language, emphasizing the need for robust grammar and spell-checking systems.

Despite the strides made in Sinhala language-based NLP and OCR research, several challenges persist. Limited linguistic resources and accurate morphological analyzers hinder system accuracy. The intricate spelling rules of Sinhala add to the complexity, necessitating hybrid models that blend rule-based and machine learning approaches. The accuracy of existing tools and systems remains below optimal levels due to the scarcity of resources for the Sinhala Language. Challenges in determining gender using a rule-based approach and the ambiguity in grammatical correctness underscore the need for ongoing research and refinement of methodologies. The unique characteristics of Sinhala, with its intricate spelling and grammar rules, call for continuous exploration and innovative solutions. Balancing comprehensiveness with efficiency and user-friendliness poses a perpetual challenge for researchers. The research community grapples with the need to enhance the robustness and effectiveness of language processing applications, ensuring they cater to the specific needs of Sinhala speakers.

In summary, the Sinhala language's rich complexity, coupled with limited linguistic resources, calls for innovative solutions in NLP and OCR research. The interplay of rule-based and machine learning approaches showcases promising strides, yet the research community faces challenges that require continued exploration and development. The dynamic landscape of Sinhala language research reflects a commitment to unraveling its intricacies and providing effective language processing solutions.

RESEARCH PROBLEM

The Sinhala language, with its complex morphology and diglossic nature—where written and spoken forms diverge—presents significant challenges for learners, especially those preparing for the Grade 5 Scholarship examination in Sri Lanka. Existing automated language learning tools often focus on singular functionalities such as spelling analysis, grammar checking, or vocabulary support. This fragmented approach fails to provide a comprehensive solution, leaving critical gaps in effectively addressing the multifaceted linguistic challenges posed by Sinhala. Consequently, students struggle to achieve proficiency, impacting their overall academic performance and confidence in using the language.

Moreover, the limited availability of robust linguistic resources and accurate morphological analyzers further exacerbates these challenges. Without a comprehensive tool, educators are unable to provide consistent and effective support tailored to the unique needs of each student. There is a pressing need for an integrated system that combines advanced Natural Language Processing (NLP) algorithms, Optical Character Recognition (OCR) technology, and Part-of-Speech (POS) tagging to enhance Sinhala language proficiency comprehensively.

Such a system must encompass real-time error detection and correction, handwriting recognition, simplified explanations of complex vocabulary, and automated assessment with instant feedback. By providing these functionalities within a single platform, the system can offer a holistic and effective learning tool for Sinhala language learners. This integrated approach would not only support immediate learning needs but also foster long-term language development, setting a new benchmark for educational technology in the Sinhala language domain. Addressing these gaps is essential for improving language education outcomes and supporting the academic success of Sinhala-speaking students.

RESEARCH GAP

The existing body of research on Sinhala language learning tools highlights several critical gaps that hinder effective language acquisition, particularly for young learners preparing for the Grade 5 Scholarship examination in Sri Lanka. Most current automated systems are fragmented, focusing on isolated functionalities such as spelling analysis, grammar checking, or dictionary access. These systems lack the integration necessary to address the full spectrum of linguistic challenges presented by the Sinhala language's complex morphology and diglossic nature.

Furthermore, the scarcity of robust linguistic resources and accurate morphological analyzers limits the effectiveness of these tools. Many available solutions do not adequately incorporate advanced technologies like Natural Language Processing (NLP) algorithms, Optical Character Recognition (OCR) technology, and Part-of-Speech (POS) tagging in a unified manner. This deficiency leaves significant gaps in real-time error detection and correction, handwriting recognition, and the provision of simplified explanations for complex vocabulary.

Additionally, existing tools often fail to offer comprehensive assessment capabilities that provide instant feedback and scoring, which are crucial for continuous learning and improvement. This lack of a holistic approach means that students and educators do not have access to an all-encompassing platform that supports diverse learning needs and promotes thorough language proficiency.

To bridge these gaps, there is a need for a comprehensive system like "SinhaLearn" that integrates multiple advanced technologies and functionalities. This system should offer real-time error detection and correction, support for handwriting recognition, simplified vocabulary explanations, and automated assessment with instant feedback. By addressing these research gaps, "SinhaLearn" can provide a robust and effective tool for enhancing Sinhala language proficiency, particularly tailored to the requirements of the Grade 5 Scholarship examination, ultimately setting a new standard in educational technology for the Sinhala language.

MAIN OBJECTIVE

The primary aim of this initiative is to innovate and enrich the preparation process for the Grade 5 Scholarship exam in Sinhala language learning through the development and deployment of the "SinhaLearn" mobile application. By harnessing the capabilities of modern technology, this application seeks to redefine how students engage with and prepare for the exam. Through a user-centric approach, සිංහLearn will offer a comprehensive platform that integrates advanced features tailored specifically to enhance Sinhala language proficiency and exam performance.

Specific Objectives
Sinhala Handwriting Capturing and Recognition on Images
This feature will empower users to capture handwritten Sinhala text through their mobile devices and seamlessly convert it into editable digital format within the app. By leveraging cutting-edge optical character recognition (OCR) technology optimized for Sinhala script, සිංහLearn aims to facilitate the effortless digitization of handwritten study materials, thereby enhancing accessibility and convenience for students preparing for the exam.

Detect and Correct Errors in Spellings and Grammar in Sinhala Sentences
SinhaLearn will integrate an intelligent spell-check and grammar correction mechanism designed specifically for Sinhala language sentences inputted by users. Leveraging sophisticated natural language processing (NLP) algorithms, this feature will automatically identify and rectify spelling errors, grammatical inaccuracies, and syntactical issues in real-time. By providing immediate feedback and suggestions for improvement, the app aims to foster improved writing skills and linguistic accuracy among students.

Generate Simplified Explanations for Sinhala Complex Words
This functionality will offer students simplified definitions and explanations for complex Sinhala words encountered within their study materials. Drawing upon comprehensive dictionaries and advanced language simplification algorithms, සිංහLearn will break down intricate vocabulary into more understandable terms, thereby facilitating deeper comprehension and retention of challenging concepts essential for the exam.

Evaluate Sinhala Answers for Past Paper Questions Based on Marking Schemes
Learn will provide an automated evaluation mechanism for Sinhala language responses to past paper questions, aligning them with predefined marking schemes. By analyzing student answers against established criteria for accuracy, completeness, and adherence to expected formats, the app aims to deliver instantaneous feedback on performance. This feature not only enhances exam readiness but also empowers students to identify and address areas for improvement effectively.

METHODOLOGY

The "SinhaLearn" mobile application represents a cutting-edge approach to Grade 5 Scholarship exam preparation in Sinhala language education. By harnessing the power of advanced technologies such as Optical Character Recognition (OCR), the app enables students to seamlessly convert handwritten Sinhala notes into digital text, facilitating easier study material management and accessibility. Natural Language Processing (NLP) algorithms further enhance the learning experience by automatically identifying and correcting spelling mistakes, grammar errors, and syntactical issues in Sinhala sentences entered by users, thereby improving language accuracy and proficiency. Moreover, the integration of neural networks within සිංහLearn allows for the automated evaluation of Sinhala language responses to past exam questions. These neural models, including recurrent neural networks (RNNs) and transformer architectures like BERT, analyze student answers against predefined marking schemes. This feature provides instant feedback on accuracy and completeness, guiding students in refining their exam preparation strategies effectively. Additionally, the application employs Random Forest, a machine learning ensemble method, to simplify explanations for complex Sinhala words encountered in study materials. By training on a corpus of Sinhala texts and corresponding simplified definitions, Random Forest generates clear and concise explanations that aid students in understanding and retaining challenging vocabulary.

In essence, the "SinhaLearn" app not only leverages these advanced technologies to streamline exam preparation but also fosters a more interactive and personalized learning environment for Grade 5 students. Through these innovative features, සිංහLearn aims to empower students with the skills and knowledge necessary to excel in the Sinhala language components of the Scholarship exam, ultimately enhancing their academic performance and educational outcomes.
